The mark.

Offset Squares · Wordmark · Lockups

Three squares climbing diagonally with depth via opacity. Reads as ascent without a chart trope, and as composition rather than a chart at any size. The wordmark is Inter Black, tracked tight, all caps. They sit on a single baseline.

The mark is legible at 16px because the squares retain rectangular silhouette without depending on stroke width. Below 16px, drop the lockup and use the favicon variant on bone. Never composite the wordmark below 14px caps height.

✓ DO · MONO

Single colour, ink or bone. Opacity ramp 0.4 / 0.7 / 1.0 stays intact.

✗ DON'T · GRADIENT

No gradients, ever. The mark is composed of solid forms.

✗ DON'T · ROTATE

Never rotate. The diagonal ascent is the mark — re-angling kills it.

✗ DON'T · STRETCH

Lock aspect ratio. Squares must remain square at every scale.

05

Voice.

How the system speaks

LEVEL is not a coach, not a cheerleader, not a buddy. It is the contract you signed with yourself, written in the second person. Brief. Quiet. Demanding. Never sentimental.

✓ ON BRAND

"Two cores left. The day qualifies when all three close."

Stated. Specific. The verb does the work.

✗ OFF BRAND

"Great job so far! 🔥 You've got this — just two more cores to crush today!"

Praise, exclamation, emoji. None of the above.

✓ ON BRAND

"Write something true. Lazy reasons survive the form, but rarely survive themselves."

Confronts the user. The contract talking, not a chatbot.

✗ OFF BRAND

"No worries! Life happens. Tell us why so we can help adjust your plan ✨"

Permissive, soft, reframes a contract as a feedback form.
